Photonic Pigments

Structural Color — Never Fades, Never Pollutes

e-skin's photonic pigments are manufactured from just two materials: aluminum and an oxide. Like butterfly wings and peacock feathers, they create color through nanostructure — not chemistry — covering the full CYM color space with zero heavy metals or biocides.

The Problem

Conventional paints are toxic and heat-absorbent

Contemporary paints contain heavy metals and biocides that pollute environments and affect human health. Traditional pigments absorb heat and transfer it to underlying layers — increasing cooling costs during peak summer electricity demand.

  • Heavy metals and biocides in every coat
  • Pigments fade with UV exposure over time
  • High thermal absorption increases HVAC load
  • Thick coatings add unwanted mass to aircraft & vehicles

The Solution

Photonic coatings that cool, last forever, and weigh almost nothing

e-skin pigments reflect 100% of infrared radiation, cooling surfaces by up to 30 degrees Fahrenheit compared to conventional paint under identical heat exposure. Individual pigments measure just 100 nanometers in thickness — a coat can be as thin as 100 nm versus 10–100 microns for conventional paint.

  • Reflects 100% of IR radiation — proven passive cooling
  • Structural color never fades or loses brightness
  • 1:1000 aspect ratio — aerospace ultralight performance
  • Two materials only: aluminum + oxide — non-toxic by design
  • Covers full CYM color gamut

Passive Cooling

30°F cooler — with zero energy cost

Side-by-side thermal imaging demonstrates a 30-degree Fahrenheit temperature difference between surfaces coated with conventional commercial paint versus e-skin photonic pigments — under identical solar heat exposure conditions.

This passive cooling effect directly reduces HVAC and air-conditioning load, cutting peak summer electricity demand for buildings, vehicles, and infrastructure.

Aerospace & Defense

Boeing-grade ultralight paint

At 100 nm per coat, e-skin photonic coatings offer a dramatic weight reduction over conventional aircraft paint. Every pound saved in aerospace directly translates to reduced fuel consumption and increased payload capacity.

Graphene Sensors

Graphene IR Sensor — Room Temperature, Uncooled

A revolutionary graphene-based infrared sensor chip that operates at room temperature without requiring external cooling systems — dramatically reducing size, weight, power, and cost versus traditional cryogenically cooled IR cameras.

Core Technology

Uncooled IR detection at room temperature

Traditional infrared cameras require bulky, expensive cryogenic cooling systems to operate. e-skin's graphene IR sensor chip delivers high-sensitivity long-wavelength infrared (LWIR) imaging at room temperature — opening IR sensing to platforms where cooling is impractical.

  • Room-temperature operation — no cryogenic cooling required
  • LWIR (long-wavelength infrared) sensitivity
  • Atom-thick graphene sensing layer
  • Multispectral detection capability
  • Suitable for integration into compact, power-constrained platforms

Graphene Power

Graphene Cell — Converts Heat to Electricity

A graphene-based thermoelectric cell that harvests waste heat from industrial sources and converts it directly to electricity — with an atom-thick active layer that makes panels extraordinarily thin, lightweight, and scalable.

Waste Heat Recovery

Captures thermal energy from industrial turbines, steam generators, and AI data centers that would otherwise be lost to the environment.

Atom-Thick Panels

Graphene is a single atom thick — enabling panels that are extraordinarily thin and lightweight compared to any competing thermoelectric technology.

Series / Parallel Config

Cells and panels connect in series or parallel, just like solar panels, allowing flexible power output scaling to match any application requirement.

AI Data Centers

Purpose-built for the massive waste heat output of modern AI compute infrastructure — turning a cooling liability into a power generation asset.

How It Works

Thermoelectric conversion at the atomic scale

e-skin's graphene power cells exploit the Seebeck effect — converting a temperature differential directly into electrical current. Because graphene is an atom thick, the resulting panels are extremely thin while maintaining high thermoelectric efficiency.

Panels can be tiled and interconnected in series or parallel configurations — analogous to solar panels — to scale from milliwatt IoT harvesting to kilowatt industrial recovery systems.

  • Industrial turbines and steam generation
  • AI & HPC data center thermal management
  • Building and infrastructure HVAC waste heat
  • Modular, scalable panel architecture
